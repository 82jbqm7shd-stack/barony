Barony of Balvill

The Barony of Balvill is a Scottish feudal barony created by Crown charter in 1630, historically associated with lands including Jura, Oronsay, and Colonsay, and held by Mark Hopking since 2025.[1][2]

Overview

The Barony of Balvill was created by Crown charter in 1630 in favour of Archibald Campbell, Lord Lorne (later Earl and Marquess of Argyll).[1][5] In modern Scots law, the barony is treated as an incorporeal heritable dignity rather than an interest requiring ownership of any particular land.[5]

The barony’s historical charter materials refer to lands and localities including Jura, Oronsay, and Colonsay.[1] Those geographic references are presented here as part of the historical description of the original grant and do not imply that the present dignity carries land ownership.

Name and historical spellings

Historical records for Scottish territorial designations frequently show orthographic variation over time. The barony appears under more than one spelling in surviving materials (including forms such as Balvill and Baluill).[1][5] In the period immediately preceding the 2025 assignation, the barony was in some contexts rendered using the modernised spelling “Barwill”; upon registration in 2025, the spelling “Balvill” was adopted to align with the earlier form of the name reflected in the original charter tradition.[5]

Creation and early confirmation

A Crown charter dated 1630 created the Barony of Balvill in favour of Archibald Campbell, Lord Lorne, and is preserved in Great Seal material supplied for this page.[1] The barony was subsequently confirmed by an Act of Parliament in 1633 (documentary copy held in the page author’s records).[3]

Modern status and relationship to land

Following the abolition of feudal tenure in Scotland, barony titles are generally treated as incorporeal heritable property. The dignity may be conveyed by assignation, while the historical territorial description remains part of the barony’s documentary identity rather than a statement of current land ownership.[5]

Assignation to Mark Hopking

The barony was assigned to Mark Hopking in 2025 (assignation instrument held in private records).[2] A certificate of registration for the dignity is also held in private records.[4]

The Scottish Barony Register publishes an “Official Register of Baronies”, in which many entries are marked as private; accordingly, it may corroborate the existence of the barony as a recognised dignity without independently evidencing proprietorship where an entry is not publicly disclosed.[7]

Stewardship

In public statements about the barony, Hopking frames the holding primarily as stewardship and custodianship of a historic dignity, rather than as a vehicle for personal styling. Where styles of address are discussed in external guidance, they are treated as conventions rather than as the subject of the barony’s historical significance.[5]
