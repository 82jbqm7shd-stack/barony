Barony of Balvill

The Barony of Balvill is a Scottish baronial dignity created by Crown charter in 1630 and held by Mark Hopking since 2025.[1][6]

This page distinguishes between information supported by published sources and material held in private records. Where evidence is unpublished or privately held, it is treated conservatively and not presented as independently verifiable.

For a detailed, source-led account of the Barony’s development from its creation in 1630 to the modern period, see History of the Barony of Balvill.

Historical origin

The Barony of Balvill was created as a feudal barony by Crown charter dated 5 August 1630, granted by King Charles I in favour of Archibald Campbell, Lord Lorne (later Earl and Marquess of Argyll).[1] The charter describes a composite barony, with its principal messuage situated in Stirlingshire and additional lands and jurisdictions recorded elsewhere, including holdings in Jura, Oronsay, and Colonsay.[1]

Parliamentary confirmation

The 1630 charter was confirmed by an Act of the Parliament of Scotland dated 28 June 1633.[2]

Continuity of holding

The barony remained associated with the Campbell family and later the house of Argyll across subsequent centuries. A detailed history of the barony is here - History of the Barony of Balvill

Transition to modern status

The abolition of feudal land tenure in Scotland (in force 2004) ended feudal landholding while preserving baronies as dignities. Since that date, the Barony of Balvill has existed as an incorporeal heritable dignity, capable of conveyance by assignation and no longer dependent on ownership of land.

References on this page to lands and jurisdiction relate to the historical form of the barony and do not imply present-day land ownership.

Assignation and registration in 2025

In 2025 the Barony of Balvill was assigned to Mark Hopking.[3] A certificate of registration for the dignity is held in the private papers of the present holder.[5]

The Scottish Barony Register publishes an “Official Register of Baronies”, in which many entries are marked as private. Inclusion on that public list may corroborate the existence of a barony as a recorded dignity without constituting public evidence of proprietorship where details are not disclosed.[6]

Name and orthography

Historical Scottish territorial designations often appear with spelling variation over time. Documentary materials include forms such as Balvill and Baluill.[1] In the period immediately preceding the 2025 assignation, the modernised spelling “Barwill” was in use in some contexts; upon registration, the form “Balvill” was adopted to reflect the earlier charter tradition.

Stewardship and usage

Contemporary commentary by the present holder frames the barony primarily as stewardship and custodianship of a historic dignity. Accepted styles of address associated with Scottish baronies are treated as conventions and are not used as running styles on this site.

Heraldry

The grant of arms held by Mark Hopking predates the 2025 assignation of the barony. Heraldic detail is treated separately on the Heraldry page.
